# Objection Handlers

Short, honest responses. Don't argue — reframe.

---

**"That's expensive."**
> "I hear that. My pricing reflects what it costs me to build something that actually works and ships on time. I'm not the right fit for every budget — and that's okay. If this package is a stretch, tell me what budget you ARE working with and I'll let you know if there's a version that makes sense, or I'll refer you to someone who fits."

---

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> "My prices are fixed per package. What I CAN do is scope down — if we pull [feature X] out, we can hit [lower price]. But I won't quietly discount and then cut corners on delivery. That's bad for both of us."

---

**"Can you start without the deposit? I'll pay when it's done."**
> "No — and here's why. The deposit isn't about trust, it's about commitment on both sides. Without it, I've seen projects drag, scope balloon, and clients ghost. The deposit locks us both in. When you pay, I start that week."

---

**"My nephew / cousin / friend said they could do it for $500."**
> "They might! And if their work is great, go with them. The difference is usually speed, support when things break, and whether it actually generates business. Happy to pass if this isn't the right fit."

---

**"Can you guarantee results? (more leads, higher ranking, etc.)"**
> "No one can honestly guarantee that and you shouldn't trust anyone who does. What I guarantee is that the SYSTEM works — the chatbot answers, the blog publishes, the site converts better than a dead page. Whether YOUR market responds is partly on me, partly on you, partly on the product you're selling. If we work together on all three, results follow."

---

**"Wordpress is free. Why can't you build on Wordpress?"**
> "I don't build on Wordpress because the sites end up slower, less secure, and harder to hand off cleanly. You get plugins breaking 6 months in. My sites load in under a second, won't get hacked, and you can host them for $0–$20/mo forever. If you want Wordpress, I'm not the right fit."

---

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Of course. What specifically are you weighing? Often I can answer it faster than you'd think."

Then actually listen. Don't push.

---

**"I need to talk to my partner/spouse."**
> "Totally. I'll send the proposal tonight so you both have the same info. What would help them say yes — would it help if I hopped on a 10-minute call with both of you?"

---

**"I want chatbot + blog + commercial + all the things. Can you bundle it cheaper?"**
> "Yes — that's what the Business Build + Growth Retainer is. Not because I'm bundling to discount, but because those three feed each other. $3,999 upfront, $4,999/mo ongoing. That's full service."

---

**"Can we pay monthly instead of 50/50?"**
> "For one-time projects under $5k, no. For retainer work, yes — that's monthly by design. For Enterprise ($7,500+), I can do 3 payments: 33% signing, 33% midpoint, 33% delivery."

---

**"What if I hate it?"**
> "You get revision rounds included — 1 for Quick Launch, 2 for Business Build, unlimited for Enterprise. If we're genuinely not clicking after revision 1, we can part ways and you keep what's been built. I'd rather lose the project than deliver something neither of us is proud of."

---

**"When can you start?"**
> "Once you sign and the deposit's in, same week. I take on 2–3 projects at a time max, so I don't overbook."

---

**Silence / ghost**

Follow up exactly twice:
- Day 3: "Hey — wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Any questions on the proposal?"
- Day 10: "Closing the file on this one if I don't hear back. Happy to revisit any time — no pressure."

Then let it go.
