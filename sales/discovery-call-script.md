# Discovery Call Script

**Goal of the call:** Decide if this is a fit. NOT to sell.
**Duration:** 30 minutes max.
**Format:** Zoom, phone, or in-person Vegas coffee — client's choice.

## Before the call

- Google the business (5 min): what they do, how big, who owns it, reviews, current site.
- Note what's broken on their current site in `clients/_scratch/<name>-notes.md`.
- Have `pricing-sheet.md` open in another tab.

## Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for making time. I've taken a quick look at [their business]. Before I jump to ideas — tell me what made you reach out this week specifically. Why now?"

*Listen for:* pain (something's broken), growth (scaling up), event (new location, new product).

## Discovery (15 min)

Work through these — don't read them, have a conversation:

1. "Walk me through what a great new customer looks like for you. How do they find you today?"
2. "What's working in your business that you wish did more of itself?"
3. "Where are you losing time or money to manual work?"
4. "If you had a full-time assistant for your online presence, what would you want them doing first?"
5. "What have you tried in the past that didn't work? Why?"
6. "What's your budget range for fixing this — or are we still figuring that out?"

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Wants a full rebuild for $300 → not a fit, refer out
- "Just make it like [competitor]" with no business goal → dig deeper or decline
- Unrealistic timeline (launch next week) → price accordingly or decline
- Wants to negotiate before I've even quoted → signal of future scope creep

## Proposal framing (5 min)

"Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend…"

Pick ONE package. Not three. Don't overwhelm.

"This is [package], it's [$X], and it takes [Y days]. I can send over a full proposal by tomorrow morning with exactly what's included and timelines. Sound good?"

## Close (3 min)

If they say yes to receiving a proposal:
- "Great — what email should I send it to?"
- "I'll include an intake form. Can you have that back to me within 3 days so we hold timeline?"
- "Once you sign and the deposit's in, we kick off that week."

If they say "need to think about it":
- "Totally fair. What would you need to see to decide?"
- Listen. Answer. Don't chase.

## After the call (within 2 hours)

1. Jot notes to `clients/_scratch/<name>-notes.md`.
2. Send thank-you email + proposal (if committed).
3. Add follow-up reminder for 3 days out if no response.

## Don't say yes to

- Equity-for-work deals (unless it's a personal friend and small scope)
- "Pay me after it launches" deals
- "Build it first, I'll tell you if I like it" deals
- Anyone who can't name what success looks like
